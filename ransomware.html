Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of virus that encrypts the files of a victim. The attacker then charges a ransom in exchange for restoring access to the files. Instructions are provided to users' in order to make payment for the decryption key. The fees can range from a few hundred dollars to thousands of dollars, and are usually paid in Cryptocurrency.

How ransomware works

There are various methods ransomware can take to infect a PC. The most common being phishing scams. The malware is included as attachments pretending to be important files. Once the file is opened, the tool begins to encrypt the victims disk. None of the files can be accessed or decrypted without paying a ransom to obtain the decryption key.

How to prevent ransomware

There are some protective measures you may take to avoid being infected with ransomware. Some of these are general security best practices.

Always keep your operating system up-to-date and install the latest security patches to ensure you have fewer vulnerabilities present.

Don't install or give it administrative rights to software unless you are completely sure its safe.

Install an antivirus, this will help detect various malware, including ransomware as they arrive, and prevent unauthorized apps from executing at all.

Back up your files on a regular basis. This will not prevent a malware attack, but it will make the harm inflicted by one far less severe.
